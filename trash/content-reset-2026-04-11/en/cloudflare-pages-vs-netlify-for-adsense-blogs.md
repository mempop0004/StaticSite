After the broad hosting comparison, the decision usually narrows to two options: `Cloudflare Pages` and `Netlify`. For an Adsense-style blog, the main question is not which platform looks better. The real question is which one is easier to operate repeatedly.

This post is the follow-up piece for that narrower decision.

## Quick answer

- choose `Cloudflare Pages` when low operating cost and simple static deployment matter most
- choose `Netlify` when previews and dashboard-driven workflows matter more
- for multiple small Adsense-focused sites, `Cloudflare Pages` is usually easier to repeat

## Why Cloudflare Pages tends to win

An Adsense blog depends on repeatable publishing more than complex product features. You want a host that makes it cheap to:

- update posts
- fix internal links
- add base pages such as privacy or contact
- repeat the same setup across multiple topic-specific blogs

That is where Cloudflare Pages feels more predictable.

## When Netlify is still the better fit

Netlify remains attractive when:

- UI work changes more often than content
- preview deploys matter a lot in the workflow
- you spend more time in the dashboard and deployment history

The tradeoff is that usage accounting can require more attention on the free tier.

## What matters for this kind of blog

For an Adsense-oriented blog, focus on three questions:

1. can you redeploy often without adding much cost
2. can you clone the setup across multiple topic-specific sites
3. can you add required base assets such as `privacy`, `contact`, and `ads.txt` without friction

By that standard, Cloudflare Pages is the simpler first pick.
