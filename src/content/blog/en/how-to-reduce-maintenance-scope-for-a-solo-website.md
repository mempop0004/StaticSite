<p>Solo sites break when the maintenance surface keeps expanding and the core loop never gets protected.</p>

<p>Before you add tools or automations, split your tasks into three buckets: trust-critical, useful, and optional.</p>

<h2>1. Define one core loop you can finish</h2>
<p>Keep a small weekly loop that protects trust: uptime check, critical forms, analytics signal, and one feed or sitemap check. If it cannot fit in 30 minutes, it is too big.</p>

<h2>2. Move slow tasks to a monthly lane</h2>
<p>Many tasks feel urgent but do not change decisions week to week. Move theme polishing, copy refresh, and minor layout tweaks to a monthly lane so they stop stealing the core loop.</p>

<h2>3. Delete tasks that never change a decision</h2>
<p>If a task never flips a decision, it is busywork. A checklist exists to protect outcomes, not habits.</p>

<h2>What to do first</h2>
<p>Label every task as trust-critical, useful, or optional. Keep the first group, limit the second, and cut the third.</p>

<p>Start with your unit page and the first operations post, then keep your weekly checklist focused on the few signals that actually move decisions.</p>
